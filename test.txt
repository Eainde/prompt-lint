1. What Makes a Role CSM

  A1 — CSM Definition (line 210)

  ▎ A CSM is a Natural Person (or NNP) with specifically assigned executive powers at the highest level of organisational management and day-to-day authority
  to act for the client entity.

Looking at the classifier prompt, banking/authorized signatories are NOT explicitly listed as "non-CSM" in a single rule. Instead, their exclusion comes from
   the combination of several rules:

  Signatory-Related Rules

  1. A1 — CSM Definition (line 219)

  Only one signatory type qualifies:
  ▎ "Authorized signatories with entity-level sole representation authority"

  This means regular banking signatories (who can sign cheques, approve payments, operate bank accounts) are not covered — only signatories who can represent
  the entire entity qualify.

  2. D2 — Negative Signal: "Signatory-only" (line 436)

  ▎ Signatory-only (no executive title) → weight: -0.25

  A pure signatory without a governance title gets penalized, making it very hard to reach the 0.70 threshold.

  3. ST1 — Signatory Weight Adjustments (line 773)
